The philosophy behind Sikhism.

A look into the core foundations of Sikhism and a deeper understanding of the primary beliefs and principles.
Guru Nanak Dev Ji when founding Sikhism instilled three core principles every Sikh should adhere to.

Naam Japna: Remembrance and meditation of God

The concept of Naam Japna is very simple, by constantly thinking about God’s virtues such as love and kindness Sikhs are able to better develop a feeling of love and kindness for humanity. Being able to see the presence of God in everyone allows the Sikh to better appreciate others and helps to prevent the thought of hurting others. Naam is given the highest priority in the Sikh faith.

Spiritual Enlightenment and becoming a Guru

Around the age of 30, it is said Guru Nanak Dev Ji gained spiritual enlightenment when he went to bathe at a small river. Having disappeared under water, it was assumed the Guru Nanak Dev Ji had drowned. After 3 days had passed, Guru Nanak emerged from the river alive saying “Na koe Hindu, Na koe Musalman – There is no Hindu, there is no Muslim”. It was at this time Guru Nanak Dev Ji was widely regarded as enlightened and was referred to as a “Guru”.

“Na koe Hindu, Na koe Musalman" – There is no Hindu, there is no Muslim

Guru Nanak Dev Ji

Until the age of 55, Guru Nanak Dev Ji travelled with his spiritual companion Bhai Mardana. Their travels took them across India, South Asia, Tibet, and Arabia. Choosing to focus on preaching a new concept Monotheism, emphasised that all human beings can have direct access to god without the need of rituals. Encouraged householders to work and make an honest living, share their food and wealth with others, to constantly remember good throughout the day. Guru Nanak Dev Ji also promoted the equality of mankind and made a special emphasis to encourage the equality of women.

The Conferring of Guruship

Guru Nanak Dev Ji’s Guruship of the Sikh faith lasted until 1529, when he passed his light onto the second Guru of the Sikhs, Guru Angad Dev Ji.
In this fashion, ten human Gurus lead the Sikh faith from the beginning until 1708. The tenth and last human Sikh Guru, Guru Gobind Singh Ji installed the Guru Granth Sahib Ji as the final Guru for the Sikh faith. “Sab Sikhan ko hukam he, Guru maneyo Granth - Every Sikh is ordered to accept the Granth as the Guru".

“Sab Sikhan ko hukam he, Guru maneyo Granth" - Every Sikh is ordered to accept the Granth as the Guru.

Guru Gobind Singh Ji

The origins of Sikhism.

A look into the origins of Sikhism, from the beginning to the present.

The origins of Sikhism trace back to the early 1500s, with the founder Guru Nanak Dev Ji. Being born into a Hindu family in the village of Talwandi, (now called Nankana Sahib, in Pakistan) Punjab.

At the time, the 2 predominant religions in India were Hinduism and Islam, with a caste system that divided the people. Anyone unfortunate to be born into a lower caste family would have found themselves relegated and segregated by society away from those from higher castes.

Definitions

Guru: In Sikhism the word Guru has very specific meaning. It means the descent of divine guidance to mankind through the Enlightened Masters.
Ji: Often used as a suffix. The term Ji is used to express more respect to a relationship.
Gurdwara: The place of worship for Sikhs. People of all faith are always welcomed in Gurdwaras.

Spiritual in Nature

Even as a child Guru Nanak Dev Ji started to question the rituals that were taking place around him. Insisting such rituals held no spiritual value
Often being scolded by his father for being considered lazy, as a child Guru Nanak Dev Ji often spent several hours in deep meditation, meditating on the divine.

Spiritual Enlightenment and becoming a Guru

Around the age of 30. One morning Guru Nanak Dev Ji went to meditate and bathe by a river with his follower Bhai Mardana. Guru Nanak Dev Ji went into the river to bathe and disappearing under water. After 3 days had passed, Guru Nanak Dev ji reappeared. It is at this point the town’s people came to agreement that Guru Nanak Dev Ji was enlightened and began to call him “Guru”.

Travels and later life

Guru Nanak Dev Ji’s travels were separated into five different mission tours, spanning around 25 years.
Settling down and spending the rest of his time in Kartarpur (now located in Pakistan), Guru Nanak continued to preach and teach, that there is only one God and the philosophy that there in only one brotherhood in humanity.

Conferring of Guruship

Guru Nanak Dev Ji’s Guruship of the Sikh faith lasted until 1529, when he passed his light onto the second Guru of the Sikhs, Guru Angad Dev Ji.
In this fashion, ten Gurus lead the Sikh faith until 1708. The tenth and last human Sikh Guru, Guru Gobind Singh Ji installed the Guru Granth Sahib Ji as the final Guru for the Sikh faith.
